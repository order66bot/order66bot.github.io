Order 66 is a multi-robot system built for the 2018 Engineering Physics robot competition

The Competition

The 2018 competition was the most complicated ever designed for ENPH 253, and also one of the most difficult. The obstacles required to overcome: A Rramp with a 6” vertical gain, a 6”  horizontal gap, a gate patrolled by rotating Stormtroopers, a 6” gap with a 2” gain in height, and a suspension bridge between two towers.

Brainstorming

We began the process with just some markers and a whiteboard. We thought of countless bizarre ideas, some which would eventually be adapted into the robot, some which lead us to our final ideas, and some which simply had to be thrown out.

Prototyping

With some simple hardboard mockups and a lot of imagination, we began preliminary testing on the robot’s modules, testing viability, chance to fail, and the stress our hardware would be under. This lead to more shifts in the design, and a few trips back to the drawing board.

Alpha Versions

After working out some kinks, the modules moved on to become powered devices, and more complicated testing began. Voltage/current testing began to become important, and the electrical aspects of the process began to be very important to not overlook.

Beta Versions

With the electrical aspects in place, and redesigns to the mechanics of the design making it more solid than ever, software began to be tested. Pickup sequences, navigation, communication – all was important as we moved closer to competition day.

Final Versions

After some small tweaks and some large ones, we began to tune the robot as a whole, ensuring it could work and behave as expected every time.

Lessons Learned

In retrospect, some prototypes could have been simplified earlier in the design process – and in general, we should have been tuning earlier to realize aspects of the robot that needed to be added much earlier than they were. Unreliability, as always, is a curse plaguing the competition.
